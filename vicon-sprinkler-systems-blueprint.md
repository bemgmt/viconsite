## Executive summary

VICON presents itself as a full-service fire protection contractor in Southern California focused on designing, engineering, installing, and maintaining fire sprinkler and related life-safety systems across commercial, industrial, and residential buildings. citeturn21view0turn3view2turn4view0 Key capabilities explicitly listed on VICON include standpipe systems (Class I/II/III), underground and overhead piping, pre-action and deluge systems for higher-risk areas, and wet/dry chemical suppression for restaurant and industrial applications. citeturn21view0

A major compliance signal on VICON’s site is testing alignment with “Title 19 and Regulation 4,” with VICON describing quarterly and annual inspections to keep systems code-compliant. citeturn21view0 In California, Title 19 inspection/testing/maintenance frequencies for water-based systems are tied to NFPA 25 (California Edition is incorporated by reference), and local authorities can require additional procedures or more frequent work. citeturn23view0 For the City of Los Angeles, Chief’s Regulation 4 is administered via the Los Angeles Fire Department’s certified tester program and includes published testing frequencies (including a Reg 4 note that pre-action, deluge, and dry pipe systems are tested annually). citeturn22view2

VICON’s public website content is strong on *scope and service categories*, but it does not publish component “models,” hydraulic performance ranges, material specs, or downloadable product datasheets in a way that supports a traditional manufacturer-style product comparison. (This is addressed explicitly in the “Missing info VICON should provide” section.) The recommended VICON partner page should therefore be positioned as a **partner-delivered sprinkler system capability page** (design/install/ITM), with a **system-type comparison matrix** that clearly marks design-dependent fields as “project-specific / not specified.” citeturn21view0turn4view0
